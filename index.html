About Me

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) in Oak Ridge, Tenneessee I work in the Radiation Transport Group,
I grew up in the D.C. metro area, before shipping off to the University of Minnesota where I double majored in chemical engineering and chemistry. As an undergraduate I gained experience in both industry and academia, working as a process engineering intern at Polar Semiconductor Inc. and as a synthetic organic chemist in the Noland Research Group. During my junior year I was first exposed to scientific computing in a numerical methods for chemical engineering course and shortly after I became interested in nuclear energy as a way to mitigate anthropogenic climate change.
These interested lead me to join the Computational Nuclear Engineering Research Group at the University of Wisconsin, where I earned my Ph.D. in nuclear engineering. As a graduate student, I had coursework in advanced mathemetics, numerical methods, algorithms, and high-performance computing. I gained real-world nuclear engineering analysis experience SHINE Medical Technologies My disseration research focused on optimizing Monte Carlo simulations for nuclear shielding problems, specifically biological dose rate calculation for nuclear fusion problems.
